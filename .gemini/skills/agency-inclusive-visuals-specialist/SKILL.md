# Inclusive Visuals Specialist

## When to Use
Load this skill during the Foundation & Structure phase of the `/design` workflow (Step 2) when Assets mode is active and generating images, to prevent embedded model biases.

## Anti-Bias Prompt Constraints
- Mandate distinct facial structures to avoid "clone faces" in diverse groups.
- Explicitly negative-prompt gibberish text or invented symbols.
- Ensure the human element is the subject, not an oversized cultural hero-symbol.

## Physical Reality Mandates
In generative video or motion logic, you must explicitly define physics:
- How clothing drapes and falls on different bodies.
- How mobility aids (wheelchairs, canes, prosthetics) interact with the environment consistently.
- How hair texture reacts to gravity and wind.

## Cultural Specificity Anchoring
- Specify location-accurate architecture (not generic skylines).
- Use correct clothing terminology for the specific region.
- Detail the appropriate lighting that correctly illuminates varied melanin levels without washing out highlights.

## Negative Prompt Library
`(no stock photo smiles, no hyper-saturated lighting, no futuristic tropes, no cloned background actors, no textbook diversity, no generic AI faces)`

## QA Review Checklist
*(Composed from source themes)*
1. **Representation Accuracy**: Does it rely on stereotypical archetypes?
2. **Artifact Avoidance**: Are there clone faces or extra limbs?
3. **Community Validation**: Would the depicted community find it authentic?
4. **Intersectional Variance**: Is there diversity in age, body type, and attire?
5. **Cultural Accuracy**: Is the architecture and environment geographically accurate?
6. **Dignity Preservation**: Is the subject presented respectfully?
7. **Agency**: Does the subject have narrative agency?
